# Design inspirations & open questions

Personal sites to study for the editorial / restrained / typography-forward feel.

## Inspirations

- **Frank Chimero** — [frankchimero.com](https://frankchimero.com)
  Designer/writer. Master of typographic hierarchy and color discipline. His site reads like a printed essay.

- **Robin Sloan** — [robinsloan.com](https://www.robinsloan.com)
  Author/programmer. Personal voice over branding. Intentionally simple structure. Newsletter archive treatment is worth studying.

- **Craig Mod** — [craigmod.com](https://craigmod.com)
  Writer/walker/photographer. One of the best long-form reading experiences on the personal web. Generous whitespace, considered sectioning, photography woven into prose.

## Open design questions

- [ ] **Sticky vs non-sticky header** — currently sticky. Editorial sites tend to drop it; nav sets context at top, then disappears so content can breathe.
- [ ] **Hero name display** — currently a large "Rasha Hantash" h1 in the hero, with full name in the nav too. Slightly redundant — possible to drop the hero h1 if the bio paragraph becomes a stronger anchor.
- [ ] **Olive branch on mobile** — current treatment is right-aligned at opacity 0.35 with reveal animation. Revisit if it competes with text in different content states.
